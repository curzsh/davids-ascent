## 2. Key Characters

### David
- Youngest of eight sons of Jesse of Bethlehem
- Described as "ruddy," handsome, with "fine appearance" (1 Sam 16:12)
- Anointed by Samuel; the Spirit of the Lord came on him from that day (16:13)
- A shepherd — his skill set and confidence comes from protecting sheep alone
- Served as Saul's harp player and armor-bearer
- His motivation before Goliath is indignation: "Who is this uncircumcised Philistine that he should defy the armies of the living God?" (17:26)
- He runs toward Goliath — not a slow approach, a sprint (17:48)

### Jesse
- An elderly man of Bethlehem ("old, advanced in years" in 17:12)
- Has eight sons; David is the eighth and youngest
- Sends David to the battle camp with bread and cheese (17:17-18)
- A domestic, parental mission — not a military one

### Eliab (the Oldest Brother)
- First son Jesse presents to Samuel; God rejects him despite impressive appearance
- At the battle camp, rebukes David: "Why have you come down here? And with whom did you leave those few sheep? I know how conceited you are and how wicked your heart is!" (17:28)
- David's calm response: "Now what have I done? Can't I even speak?" (17:29)

### King Saul
- A troubled, fearful king — the Spirit of the Lord has departed (16:14)
- Head and shoulders taller than any Israelite — his fear of Goliath is ironic
- Doubts David but offers his own armor in desperate hope
- His armor: a bronze helmet, a coat of armor (17:38)

### Goliath
- Champion of Gath — a single-combat representative
- Height: six cubits and a span (~9 feet 9 inches)
- Armor described in 17:4-7:
  - Bronze helmet
  - Coat of scale armor: 5,000 shekels of bronze (~125 pounds)
  - Bronze greaves on his legs
  - Bronze javelin slung on his back
  - Spear shaft "like a weaver's beam," iron point: 600 shekels (~15 pounds)
  - A shield-bearer who walked ahead of him
- Taunt: "Am I a dog, that you come to me with sticks?" (17:43)
- "He cursed David by his gods" (17:43)
- Died from a single stone to the forehead; "fell facedown on the ground" (17:49)

### Samuel
- Last judge of Israel and a prophet
- His anointing of David is the theological foundation: "the Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart." (16:7)

## 3. Biblical Details in the Game

### The Five Smooth Stones (1 Sam 17:40)
"He chose five smooth stones from the stream, put them in the pouch of his shepherd's bag and, with his sling in his hand, approached the Philistine."

- River-worn stones are aerodynamically consistent — prized by slingers
- The number five is specific and intentional
- Used in: Stage 4 intro and outro, "Five Smooth Stones" upgrade

### David's Harp / Music (1 Sam 16:14-23)
- David played the kinnor (a small portable lyre) to soothe Saul
- "Whenever the spirit from God came on Saul, David would take up his lyre and play. Then relief would come to Saul." (16:23)
- Not currently in-game — potential future mechanic or blessing

### The Valley of Elah
- Philistines camped between Socoh and Azekah, at Ephes Dammim
- Israel camped in the Valley of Elah; each army on a hillside
- The brook David took stones from runs through this valley

### 40 Days of Taunting (1 Sam 17:16)
- "For forty days the Philistine came forward every morning and evening"
- 40 is a number of trial throughout the Bible
- Referenced in Stage 3 outro

### The Killing Blow (1 Sam 17:49-51)
- "The stone sank into his forehead, and he fell facedown on the ground"
- David then took Goliath's own sword and cut off his head (17:51)
- "Facedown" mirrors the posture of submission/worship — theologically significant
- Used in: Stage 5 post-dialogue and Victory screen

## 5. Enemy Theming

### Wilderness (Stages 1-2)
- Lion, bear, wolf — directly from 1 Samuel 17:34-36
- Snakes — not in text but consistent with Near Eastern shepherding

### Philistines (Stages 3-5)
- Shield-Bearer — directly from 1 Samuel 17:7 and 17:41 (Goliath's personal shield-bearer)
- Scouts/Dashers — implied by army positioning in 17:1-3
- Soldiers — "Philistine warriors" from the camp

### Goliath
- "Champion of Gath" — single-combat representative
- His shield-bearer is the Stage 5 Shield enemy — narratively justified
